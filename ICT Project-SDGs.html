 Now, a quick introduction for those who are clueless about SDGs~


The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), also known as the Global Goals, were adopted by the United Nations in 2015 as a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure that by 2030 all people enjoy peace and prosperity. 17 SDGs were thus integrated.

SDG 14:Life Below Water: Quick Intro

Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development.



Healthy oceans and seas are essential to our existence.They cover 70 percent of our planet and we rely on them for food, energy and water. Yet, we have managed to do tremendous damage to these precious resources. We must protect them by eliminating pollution and overfishing and immediately start to responsibly manage and protect all marine life around the world.

Problems of SDG 14
Overfishing

devastates fish populations and wildlife, breaks down the food web and degrades habitats

Pollution

damage to the environment, to the health of all organisms, and to economic structures worldwide

Ocean Acidification

create conditions that eat away at the minerals used by oysters, clams, lobsters, shrimp, coral reefs, and other marine life to build their shells and skeletons.

Eutrophication

a decrease in biodiversity, an increase in toxicity of the water body, and a change in species dominance.
